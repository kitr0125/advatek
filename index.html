01Purpose

This document outlines the standard operating procedures for Advatek Technology Solutions' information security risk assessment, business continuity planning, cybersecurity compliance, and related operational policies. It is the authoritative reference for all personnel managing or supporting Advatek's infrastructure and client environments.

02Scope

This SOP applies to all personnel, systems, services, and data managed by Advatek Technology Solutions — including security operations, client-facing infrastructure, remote and on-site support, and all managed endpoints regardless of location.

03Information Security Risk Assessment

Objective: To identify, assess, and manage information security risks consistently across the organization.

1
Define Scope — Identify the systems, services, or business functions to be assessed.
2
Asset Inventory & Classification — Catalog assets and assign CIA ratings.
3
Threat & Vulnerability Identification — Document threats and weaknesses from logs, scans, and intelligence.
4
Risk Scoring — Assign Likelihood and Impact ratings; determine Inherent Risk via matrix.
5
Risk Treatment — Accept, Mitigate, Transfer, or Avoid each identified risk.
6
Residual Risk Evaluation — Assess remaining risk after controls are applied.
7
Documentation — Maintain a central Risk Register with audit-ready records.
8
Review Cycle — Reassess annually or following significant changes.

Risk Assessment Owner: Kit Riños  ·  Oversight: Raffie Freiden

07Anti-Malware & System Hardening
7.1 Anti-Malware Controls
  • Real-Time Monitoring — Continuously enabled for all endpoints.
  • Scheduled Scan Tasks — Automated threat detection calibrated by client environment. Standard environments run a daily quick scan and weekly full scan. Regulated environments (healthcare) run a nightly full scan. All missed scans execute automatically when the endpoint comes back online.
  • Signature Updates — Virus definitions updated every hour from Bitdefender's servers, ensuring current threat coverage across all managed endpoints.
  • Policy Enforcement — Managed via GravityZone central dashboard by device role and client environment. Authoritative configuration source: GravityZone Healthcare Hardening (April 2026) document.
  • Review Cycle — Anti-malware settings and logs are reviewed quarterly or following any significant incidents. Configurations are documented within the GravityZone management console and subject to periodic audit.
7.2 System Configuration & Hardening
  • Baseline Standards — Workstations (Windows 10/11), servers, and network devices follow CIS Controls v8, CIS Benchmarks, and Microsoft Security Baselines.
  • Deployment — Hardened configurations are deployed via Syncro RMM at provisioning and, where applicable, via Group Policy Objects (GPOs) and standardized system images. Configurations are applied consistently across all managed endpoints.
  • Controls Enforced — Baseline hardening covers access control policies, protocol security, credential protection, inactivity lock enforcement, remote access security, attack surface reduction, and audit logging. Specific controls are calibrated to the security requirements of each client environment.
  • Ongoing Compliance Monitoring — Automated weekly checks verify that security configurations remain in place across all managed endpoints. Any deviation from the established baseline generates an alert for immediate review and remediation.
  • Network Devices — Configured using vendor security templates with secure protocols (SSH, HTTPS), restricted ports, and administrative access logging enabled.
  • Documentation & Review — All hardening standards are formally documented and reviewed quarterly or after changes to operating systems, platforms, or applicable best practice guidelines. Authoritative sources: SMB Standard Hardening and Syncro Healthcare Hardening (April 2026) documents.
08Vulnerability Management
8.1 Severity Classification (CVSS-aligned)
Critical

Immediate remediation required. Exploitation is likely or occurring.

High

Remediate within SLA. Significant exposure risk to client systems.

Medium

Scheduled remediation. Contextual risk reviewed before prioritization.

Low

Address in routine maintenance cycle. Minimal exploitation risk.

8.2 Triage & Reclassification Authority

Advatek reserves the right to upgrade or downgrade the severity of a detected vulnerability based on business impact, exploitability, or compensating controls. Any reclassification must be documented in the Risk Register or Vulnerability Tracker with the following details:

  • Vulnerability ID or description
  • Original CVSS rating · Adjusted severity · Justification
  • Assigned owner · Target remediation date
8.3 Response Workflow
1
Log & Track — Issue entered into vulnerability tracking system.
2
Assign Ownership — Responsible engineer or team assigned.
3
Mitigation or Remediation — Controls applied per severity SLA.
4
Validation — Fix validated via retest or third-party confirmation.
5
Closure — Final status recorded in Risk Register.
8.4 Responsibilities · 8.5 Review Cycle
  • Security & Engineering Team — Perform assessments, assign severity, validate mitigations.
  • Risk Assessment Owner — Oversees documentation and ensures all steps are followed.
  • Executive Oversight — Ensures timely and effective response to high-risk vulnerabilities.
  • Review: Process reviewed annually or following a major incident or technology change.

9.1 Incident Classification
Low

Minor policy violations or isolated user issues.

Medium

Malware detected, unusual login activity, or partial system disruption.

High

Unauthorized access, compromised credentials, or significant anomalies.

Critical

Confirmed breach, ransomware, system-wide outage, or regulatory violation.

9.3 Response Workflow
1
Detection — All suspicious activity logged immediately via Bitdefender, Syncro, O365, or user report.
2
Classification & Escalation — Notify Incident Response Lead for Medium–Critical events.
3
Containment — Isolate affected systems. Disable compromised accounts.
4
Eradication — Remove malware. Revoke credentials, apply required patches.
5
Recovery — Restore from known-good backups. Monitor for reinfection.
6
Notification — Clients notified within 24 hours of confirmed breach.
7
Post-Incident Review — Within 5 business days. Update SOPs. Log in Risk Register.

  • All incidents logged in Risk Register; records retained minimum 3 years for compliance and audits.
10Personal Cloud Storage & Data Egress

Installation of personal cloud storage clients on managed endpoints is prohibited by default. Exceptions require written approval from the client's designated authority.

10.1 Scope

Applies to all managed workstations, laptops, and endpoints under Advatek's administration. Personal cloud storage applications include, but are not limited to: Apple iCloud Drive, Google Drive (personal), Dropbox (personal), OneDrive Personal, and any similar file synchronization service tied to a personal or non-organizational account.

10.3 Risk Basis
Why this policy exists. Files synced to personal cloud accounts leave the managed environment and are no longer subject to organizational access controls, audit logging, or retention policies. In regulated environments (healthcare, legal, financial), synchronization of client records to personal accounts may constitute a breach of confidentiality obligations under HIPAA, attorney-client privilege, or contractual data handling requirements. Revocation of access at offboarding does not guarantee removal of data already synced. Personal accounts cannot be monitored, audited, or wiped remotely.
10.4 Approval & Installation Workflow
1
End user submits a request with business justification.
2
Advatek escalates to client's designated authority for written approval.
3
Approval documented: authority, date, scope, and application name.
4
Installation performed by Advatek engineering using admin credentials only.
5
Application and approval logged in client's asset or change log.
10.5 Restrictions & 10.6 Non-Compliance
  • Only work-related files may be synchronized; client data remains subject to the client's classification and handling policies.
  • Installations in regulated environments (HIPAA, legal, financial) are flagged as elevated risk and documented in the Risk Register.
  • Approval is revocable at any time by the client authority; Advatek will uninstall upon written revocation.
  • Unapproved installations are treated as AUP violations; Advatek may remove the application and escalate to client leadership without further notice.
10.7 Responsibilities
  • Operations Manager (Kit Riños) — Receives and escalates requests; documents approvals and installations; flags elevated-risk environments.
  • Client Designated Authority — Reviews and approves or denies requests; bears responsibility for data governance decisions within their organization.
  • Executive Oversight (Raffie Freiden) — Reviews flagged cases in regulated environments; approves any policy exceptions.
